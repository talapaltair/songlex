Why songs?

Authentic input that makes vocabulary memorable

Songs combine rhythm, emotion, repetition, and real language use. In SongLex, they work as pedagogical texts for lexical competence, not as entertainment only.

Authentic input

Students hear natural pronunciation, rhythm, and vocabulary in context.

Contextual learning

Words are connected with unit topics, emotions, and meaningful situations.

Vocabulary recycling

Each song includes useful expressions, collocations, chunks, and a mini quiz.

Communication output

Students use target vocabulary in speaking, writing, reflection, and checkpoints.

About the project

A dissertation practical product

SongLex Grade 11 was designed for the dissertation "Enhancing High School Students' Lexical Competence through Song Materials." It follows the unit topics of Solutions Kazakhstan Edition Grade 11 and provides two song-based modules for every unit.

The website uses a communicative-cognitive and competence-based approach. Students first recognise vocabulary, then understand meaning in context, practise collocations, and finally use new words in speaking and writing.

Methodological basis

  • Communicative-cognitive approach
  • Competence-based approach
  • Contextual vocabulary learning
  • Pre-listening, while-listening, post-listening model
  • Receptive and productive vocabulary development

How it works

A clear learning route for students and teachers

  1. 1Open the unit page connected with your coursebook topic.
  2. 2Read the vocabulary focus and learning objectives.
  3. 3Listen to Song 1 and complete the task sequence.
  4. 4Listen to Song 2 and complete the task sequence.
  5. 5Complete the vocabulary checkpoint.
  6. 6Add useful words to your vocabulary notebook.
  7. 7Use new vocabulary in speaking or writing.

Lesson timing

Use 40-45 minutes per song: lead-in, prediction, first listening, vocabulary noticing, post-listening output, and reflection.

Classroom use

Use the website as supplementary material after the coursebook unit or before vocabulary revision.

Vocabulary strategy

Ask students to infer meaning from context before checking dictionaries. Recycle vocabulary in later lessons.

Song selection and copyright-friendly use

Use songs that match the coursebook topic, contain useful B1-B2 vocabulary, are age-appropriate, and are available through official platforms. Do not publish full lyrics or upload copied audio. Build tasks around vocabulary, collocations, short phrases, paraphrase, and student-created output.

Differentiation tips

For weaker students, pre-teach five words, provide word banks, and reduce writing length. For stronger students, add synonym work, connotation questions, and student-created mini quizzes.
